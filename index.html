Privacy Policy

Last updated — July 7, 2026

My Auto is a media player for Android and Android Auto. This policy explains, in plain terms, how the app handles your information.

The short version: the app does not collect, store, sell, or transmit any personal data to the developer or any third party. There are no analytics, advertising, or tracking libraries. Your media, settings, and playback history stay on your device.

What the app accesses on your device

To work as a media player, My Auto accesses the following locally, without sending it anywhere:

  • Media and files — the audio, video, and playlists you open or browse, so they can be played.
  • Network streams (IPTV / URLs) — addresses you enter are used only to fetch and play the stream you asked for, directly between your device and that server.
  • Settings and playback history — kept on your device to remember preferences and resume where you left off.

Optional features that reach third-party services

Some features contact outside services only when you choose to use them. When you do, that provider's own privacy policy applies:

  • Google Drive — if you sign in to browse and play files from your Drive. Handled by Google.
  • Subtitle translation — if you request a translation, subtitle text may be sent to the configured translation service to return the result.
  • Cast / DLNA — if you cast media to another device on your network.

The developer neither receives nor retains any data from these services.

Permissions

My Auto requests only the Android permissions needed to play media — access to media and files, and network access for streaming. Each permission is used solely for its corresponding playback feature.

Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Any change will be posted on this page with a revised “last updated” date.
